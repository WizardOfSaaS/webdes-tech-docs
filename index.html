Introduction

CSS Child Selectors are pseudo-classes that allow you to apply styles to elements that are direct children of a specified parent.

There are various selectors available to target the child elements within a parent.

First child

The :first-child selector targets the first child within the parent.

Last child

The :last-child selector targets the last child within the parent.

nth child

This selector takes an argument, n.

The :nth-child(n) selector targets the nth-child within the parent.

Examples:

  • nth-child(3) selects the 3rd child
  • nth-child(5) selects the 5th child
  • nth-child(9) selects the 9th child
Even or Odd children

The :nth-child-even selector targets the even children in the parent.

The :nth-child-odd selector targets the odd children in the parent.

A neat use case is to alternate the colors of table rows.

Examples:

  • :nth-child-even selects even children, viz. 2, 4, 6, and so on
  • :nth-child-odd selects odd children, viz. 1, 3, 5, and so on
